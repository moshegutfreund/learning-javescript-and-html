    Notable Figures in Aritifical Intelligence

  1. NVIDIA

    Nvidia is an American company that designs graphics processing units for gaming and professional markets. They are considered a trailblazer in the world of AI and have encouraged thousands of AI startups through their Inception Program, making strides in the world of autonomous vehicles, social innovation and even healthcare.

  2. Elon Musk

    Elon Musk, born in South Africa in 1971, has founded numerous companies, such as Tesla, SpaceX, PayPal, and the Boring Company. They have made humongous strides in the world of Artifical Intelligence. He's founded a non-profit organization, OpenAI, looking to promote friendly artifial intelligence. He's also created NeuroLink a company which is in the process of developing a way to integrate AI into human brains.

  3. Andrew Ng

    Andrew Ng, started off as the head of Google Brain. He's led multiple companies in their pursuit of AI and is also an adjunct professor at Standford University. One of the greatest acheivements however of Andrew Ng is his co-founding of Coursera. By doing so he's allowed several million people to learn courses for free, one of which he teaches. In his 2019 course "AI for Everyone", he takes on a non-technical approach to help people understand AI in a much more general and big picture way.

  4. Alan Turing

    Alan Turing was an English inventor, mathematician, and computer scientist. During WW2 he worked hard to crack the enigma and it was his Turing Machine that became one of the first computers ever. His work would later lead to the creation of AI in the future. His test, the Turing Test, is now used as a standard of whether or not a computer is "thinking". He states that a computer is considered to be truly thinking when it can carry a conversation with a human via a printer.

  5. Dorian Selz

    Dorian Selz is the Co-founder of Squirro. Squirro is meant to help businesses analyze data more efficiently using AI to decode, sort and then make the best recommendations for action. By streamling this process, businesses can make better decisions to promote their work.
